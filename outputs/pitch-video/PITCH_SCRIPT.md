## Short Spoken Version For Live Presentation

Borealis is a MEZO-powered MUSD capital router for the Mezo ecosystem.

A DAO can help a community decide. Borealis helps operators turn that signal
into allocation: choose gauges, route capital, and verify the result onchain.

The problem is simple: most governance tools stop at proposals and votes, but
Mezo still needs a clear path from project request, to MEZO signal, to MUSD
movement, to onchain proof.

Borealis creates that path. Builders apply for gauges. Operators allocate MUSD
across those gauges. MEZO-powered signals make priorities explicit. And the app
anchors every final capital route to Mezo testnet proof.

In the demo, an ecosystem operator opens Borealis, enters the capital router,
connects with Passport when ready, adjusts MUSD allocations across gauges, and
verifies the contract, transaction, and allocation event on Mezo testnet.

This fits the MEZO Utilization track because it gives MEZO a visible job:
coordinate capital. It also increases MUSD velocity, gives builders a more
accountable funding path, and gives Mezo a reusable primitive for grants,
liquidity incentives, and ecosystem governance.
